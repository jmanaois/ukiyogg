ProjectJIM

August 2022 - Present
iOS App UX/UI Design Swift

ProjectJIM is an iOS application that I am collaborating on with 2 Computer Engineers for our Senior Project at SFSU. The application analyzes user movement in order to assist with the user's workout routines. The application also serves as a guide, showing the user when they worked out and adjusting their workouts for their future sessions.

The key features of the app are the Repetition Counter, Workout Schedule, and Workout Customization. The Repetition Counter utilizes data received from the iPhone's gyroscope sensors and digital signal processing in order to analyze the user's movement. For our app, we are first configuring the data for a dumbell curl. The Counter then will show the user the amount of repetitions they have completed and how many to complete the set. After the workout, the app will take user feedback such as if the workout was too easy/challenging and will scale/dial back future workouts so the user can progress towards their goals. Lastly, the Workout Schedule shows the user when they worked out through a calendar view and also shows when Scheduled Rest Days should occur.

For ProjectJIM, I am currently managing the application's UI design. We want the app to be as easy to use as possible, so a minimalist-style interface seemed like the route to go on. Referencing other workout apps such as 24GO, Apple Fitness +, and Nike Training Club, we decided to only implement our core features into the app, making it easier to navigate and not look as cluttered.
